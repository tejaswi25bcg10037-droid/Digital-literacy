Case Study: Phishing Attack on a Banking Customer
Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted entities to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or credit card numbers. It typically unfolds in a step-by-step manner:
- Bait Creation – The attacker designs a fake email or website that closely resembles a legitimate institution, often a bank or e-commerce platform.
- Delivery – The phishing email is sent to thousands of users, urging them to take immediate action (e.g., “Your account will be blocked unless you verify your details”).
- Deception – The victim clicks on the malicious link, which redirects them to a fraudulent login page.
- Data Harvesting – Once the victim enters their credentials, the attacker captures the information.
- Exploitation – The stolen data is used to access accounts, transfer funds, or commit identity theft.
A real-world example occurred in 2023 when Indian banking customers received emails mimicking the State Bank of India. Victims were asked to “update KYC details” via a link. Many unsuspecting users entered their account numbers and passwords, which were then misused to siphon money from their accounts.
